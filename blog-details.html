The Art of Searing Wagyu: Tips from Our Executive Chef

Chef Marcus Thorne
May 15, 2026 8 min read

Wagyu beef is one of the most prized proteins on the planet. Its extraordinary marbling, buttery texture, and umami-rich depth of flavour make it an entirely different cooking challenge compared to conventional beef. Searing it correctly is an art form.

Understanding the Marbling

The key to wagyu's signature richness is its intramuscular fat — the intricate web of white fat laced throughout the muscle tissue. This fat melts at a lower temperature than standard beef fat, which means your searing technique must be adapted accordingly.

"A great wagyu steak isn't cooked — it's persuaded. You never force it. You listen to the sizzle and let the fat tell you when it's ready."

— Chef Marcus Thorne, FuelUp Executive Chef

The 5 Golden Rules

01

Rest at Room Temperature

Take the steak out of the fridge 30-45 minutes before cooking. Cold wagyu contracts and cooks unevenly.

02

Dry the Surface Completely

Pat with paper towels until bone dry. Surface moisture is the enemy of the Maillard reaction crust.

03

Season Minimally

Coarse sea salt and cracked black pepper only. The beef's own flavour is the star — don't compete with it.

04

Medium-High Heat, Cast Iron Pan

Not screaming hot like standard beef. Medium-high on cast iron gives even heat distribution and a clean sear without burning the melting fat.

05

Rest After Cooking

Always rest for at least half the cooking time. Cutting too soon releases precious juices that should redistribute through the meat.
